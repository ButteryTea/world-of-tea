The 5 Basic Types of Tea

White Tea

Originally from Fujian. China, white tea is the least processed of all teas due to its underdeveloped nature from being harvested from the young leaves of plants. Read More.

Green Tea

One of the most popular teas worldwide, green tea is an unoxidized tea due to it being heated immediately after being harvested from the plant. Read More.

Oolong Tea

Also known as Wulong tea, oolong tea is a semi-oxidized tea, and its colored infusion ranges between green and black tea, depending on its preparation. Read More.

Black Tea

The most popular type of tea and known for 85% of total tea consumption in Western culture, the black tea is a fully-oxidized tea dried for preparation. Read More.

Herbal Tea

Also known as herbal infusions or tisanes, herbal tea doesn't come from tea leaves unlike other types of tea - but it is made from dried herbs, fruits, and flowers. Read More.
