Different Uses for Tea

Explore the different uses of tea in Western and Eastern cultures.

Around the World

Tea In Western Culture

Potpourri

Socialization

Productivity

Potpourri

With the French people discovering potpourri to experience better scents and fragrances in their surroundings, the highly absorbent nature and various aromas of the tea leaves makes it perfect to be used as a potpourri.

Just pick your favorite type of tea, pour them into a bowl, and place them in different places inside the house to infuse and brighten your environment with the aroma of your favorite tea.

Socialization

In the western world, tea became a medium to socialize in gatherings and formal parties - which is why tea parties often have fine porcelain, bone China, or silver tea sets with matching cups and saucers to impress guests.

Tea is also used as a medium for communication and forming connections or relationships in social circles in Western culture through its preparation and presentation to visitors.

Productivity

Before the arrival of tea in Europe, productivity is low due to the negative health effects of beer on workers. Hence, the arrival of tea in the Western world contributed to the worker's productivity by working longer hours and gaining more profit.

Compared to beer, the tea's preparation of having to boil water prevented water-borne diseases such as cholera, dysentery, and typhoid from workers - making tea a replacement for beer in labor society.

Tea In Eastern Culture

Medicine

Cooking

Ceremonies

Medicine

Before tea was discovered to be consumed as a drink, the Chinese primarily use tea as traditional medicine due to its numerous medicinal properties in treating various diseases.

The tea's health benefits of having anti-oxidants, anti-inflammatory, immuno-regulatory, and cardiovascular protective qualities make it a vital ingredient in Chinese medicine in strengthening and improving a person's health.

Cooking

Ever since the ancient times, the Chinese have discovered using tea as an ingredient and seasoning in cooking too - aside from using it as a beverage or medicine.

The tea has a subtle, distinct flavor that provides a new taste to a dish - as shown in Chinese tea leaf eggs. The hard-boiled eggs were simmered in a mixture of water, soy sauce, and tea leaves, and peeling the shell after shows a dark, flavorful broth on the egg that tastes like tea.

Ceremonies

Aside from tea being used for medicine, beverage, and seasoning, it is also used in Eastern culture for ceremonies - such as the Japanese tea ceremony in Japan.

A Japanese tea ceremony is an art form of intricate movements performed in a strict order and appreciated by the guest - and they often use matcha powdered green tea (otemae) with traditional tea ceremony tools.
